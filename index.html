Darrin "X" Frazier

Go-Go Architect / Musician / Cultural Advocate

A lifelong architect of Washington, DC's Go-Go culture, with over 40 years of hands-on experience shaping the music, the movement, and the community around it. From stage crew to keyboardist, producer to advocate—this is the story of how Go-Go became more than just music, and how one person helped carry its voice forward.

The Journey

Every role taught me something essential. Every setback revealed a truth. Here's how it all unfolded.

Early 1980s - 1991

The Beginning: Stage Crew to Technician

It started with Experience Unlimited (EU) in the early '80s—stage crew work that offered a ground-level view of Go-Go at its absolute peak. By 1985, I was working as a stage technician for Rare Essence, learning the mechanics behind the magic, the technical foundations that made the music hit different.

  • Stage crew for Experience Unlimited during Go-Go's golden era
  • Stage technician for Rare Essence starting in 1985
  • Witnessed and supported Go-Go culture at its creative height
  • Built deep understanding of technical production and live performance
1991 - 2000s

On Stage: Rare Essence Keyboardist

In 1991, I officially joined Rare Essence as a keyboardist—a role I held for over a decade. This wasn't just performing; it was creating. I earned production credits on some of the band's most influential 1990s records, helping shape the sound that defined an era.

  • Keyboardist for Rare Essence for over 10 years
  • Production credits: Work the Walls, Must Be Like That, Ah-Oh
  • Production credits: Body Snatchers, Overnight Scenario
  • Contributed to the sound that defined 1990s Go-Go
2001

The 911 Era: Honoring the Godfather

Continued the musical journey with the band 911, performing alongside Donnell Floyd at the legendary Chuck Brown Birthday Celebration Live at the 9:30 Club. This was about honoring the roots while pushing the culture forward.

  • Performed with 911 alongside Donnell Floyd
  • Featured at Chuck Brown Birthday Celebration at 9:30 Club
  • Continued to advance Go-Go's musical evolution
2003 - 2011

Innovation: Creating the Bounce Beat Movement

Partnered with Reginald "Polo" Burrell of TCB and entrepreneur Ben Abba to create and market the Bounce Beat sound—a Go-Go subgenre that sparked the "Beat Your Feet" dance movement. When Wale licensed TCB's "Bait" for his Ambition album through Warner Music Group in 2011, Go-Go reached new heights. I helped make that deal happen.

  • Co-created the Bounce Beat subgenre with TCB and Ben Abba
  • Sparked the national "Beat Your Feet" dance movement
  • Facilitated Wale's licensing of TCB's "Bait" for Warner Music Group
  • Brought Go-Go to mainstream national attention
2015 - Present

Cultural Architect: Advocacy & Innovation

Go-Go became more than music—it became a tool for connection and advocacy. Co-owner of Go-GoRoyalty.com, a streaming platform for Go-Go music, I've worked to expand the culture's reach globally. Through collaborations with Moechella founder Yaddiya and the "Don't Mute DC" movement, I helped position Go-Go as a unifying force. Instrumental in helping the Go-Go Museum and Cafe get open, I reached out using my friendships and respect within the Go-Go community to secure representation and participation from musicians who helped elevate Go-Go music and its culture. I worked with Monumental Sports to support the Capital City Go-Go naming, and partnered with Councilmember Kenyan McDuffie to secure COVID-19 relief for Go-Go bands. Now pioneering Go-Go in 360° virtual experiences, digital archiving, and digital marketing—giving the culture an evolving voice that reaches far beyond DC.

  • Collaborated with Moechella and "Don't Mute DC" movement
  • Instrumental in opening the Go-Go Museum and Cafe
  • Leveraged community relationships to secure Go-Go representation
  • Co-owner of Go-GoRoyalty.com 24/7 streaming platform
  • Helped unify Go-Go community for Capital City Go-Go naming
  • Secured COVID-19 relief funds for Go-Go bands in 2021
  • Pioneering 360° experiences, digital archiving, digital marketing, and AI for Go-Go

What I've Learned

Forty years in Go-Go taught me this: you can't preserve culture from the outside looking in. You preserve it by living it, by showing up for the community when it matters, by using every connection you've built to open doors for the next generation. From stage crew to the museum—it's all been about keeping Go-Go alive, relevant, and respected.

Culture is Living

Go-Go survives because it adapts. From Bounce Beat to streaming platforms to digital marketing and AI—innovation doesn't dilute tradition, it extends it.

Community First

Every move I've made—from "Don't Mute DC" to COVID relief—starts with the people. The bands, the fans, the city. They're the heartbeat.

Legacy Through Action

40+ years in, I'm still building. Still connecting. Still making sure Go-Go's voice gets louder, not quieter. That's how you honor what came before.

What I Create and Manage

Platforms, initiatives, and projects that give Go-Go culture a voice, a home, and a future.

I'm a digital innovator dedicated to amplifying Go-Go culture for a new era. My passion project is a suite of platforms: Go-Go VR for immersive concerts, GoGo DB as the genre's AI historian, Go-Go Maps for navigating the scene, and Crankington, DC for wearable culture. Together, they use technology to archive, connect, and celebrate the sound of DC.
